Getting Started

What Is EnglishConnect 3?

EnglishConnect 3 is an English course designed to help you achieve increased spiritual and temporal self-reliance. This course is designed to help you:

  • Increase your faith in Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ.
  • Become an agentive learner.
  • Increase your English proficiency to achieve your education and employment goals.

EnglishConnect 3 has two parts:

  • Weekly Gathering
  • Online Learning Resources & Learner Manual

How Can I Use This Course to Improve My English Proficiency?

To achieve intermediate-high level proficiency, you need to master these four functions:

  • Ask & Answer Questions
  • Describe
  • Narrate
  • Negotiate

You will practice the four functions at the Weekly Gathering.

On this website, you will practice the functions to improve your Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing. You will practice each function with multiple topics. Start at Level 1 and work to complete Level 4.

How Do I Know If I Am Improving?

Track Your Progress:

When you complete an online practice activity, you will receive a score.

  • Record your scores on your scoring sheet (in the Learner Manual).
Self-Evaluate:

At the end of each practice, take time to self-evaluate.

  • Review your scores and your goals. Are you improving?
  • Choose one thing you will try to improve in the next practice.
  • Write your goal on your Scoring Sheet.

How Do I Move to the Next Level?

In the online activities, you will move from Level 1 to Level 4. At each level, you will practice the functions with multiple topics.

To move to the next level, you must achieve 75% on advanced questions, and 90% on intermediate questions for four topics.

  • After you complete the practice for each topic, record your scores on your scoring sheet.

When you achieve 75% on the advanced questions, and 90% on intermediate questions for 4 topics, you are ready to take the Proficiency Check.

When you pass the Proficiency Check, you will go to the next level and begin the process again.

How Can I Be Successful?

You will be most successful as you prayerfully seek the gift of tongues, apply the principles of being an agentive learner, and attend the Weekly Gathering.

To learn English, you must study and practice every day. Try to study and practice for 10 hours each week.

  • Record your daily study time in the Tracking Tool (in the Learner Manual).

Being an Agentive Learner

What Is an Agentive Learner?

An agentive learner chooses to act, and not be acted upon (2 Nephi 2:14-16). An agentive learner has a vision for who they will become, and consistently works toward that vision.

You can be an agentive learner. You are a child of God. You have divine nature and potential. As a child of God, you are endowed with power to act, and not be acted upon (2 Nephi 2:14-16). You can decide who you will become and what you will achieve in this life.

Learning English isn’t easy, but when you choose to be an agent of your own learning, you can accomplish your goals!

As you study, strive to apply the principles outlined below.

Principles of Being an Agentive Learner

  • Exercise Faith (Philippians 4:13)
  • Take Responsibility (2 Nephi 2:14-16)
  • Teach One Another (D&C 88:77-79)
  • Press Forward (2 Nephi 31:20)
  • Apply Effective Methods (D&C 109:7-9; 88:119)
  • Self-Evaluate (D&C 7:3-4)

How Do I Improve My Ability to Be an Agent Learner?

  • Study the six principles listed above.
  • Apply the principles in your daily study.
  • Each week, evaluate your progress. Use the progress tracker
    • Record your experiences in your Learning Journal (in the Learner Manual)

Weekly Gathering

The Weekly Gathering is the most important part of this course.

Each week, you will attend a Weekly Gathering with your classmates, and two Pathway Missionaries. At the Weekly Gathering, you will have opportunities to learn and teach. Each week, one student will be the Lead Student. The Lead Student will follow a lesson plan to lead the learning activities.

To prepare for the Weekly Gathering:

  • Download the Gathering Preparation Workbook
  • Read the introduction. Follow the instructions in the introduction.

If you are the Lead Student:

  • Download the Lead Student Gathering Workbook
  • Read the introduction. Follow the instructions in the introduction.
