Getting Started

What Is EnglishConnect 3?

EnglishConnect 3 is an English course designed to help you achieve increased spiritual and temporal self-reliance. This course can help you:

  • Get the gospel down into your hearts
  • Become an agentive learner
  • Increase your English proficiency to qualify for employment and academic opportunities that require advanced-level proficiency

EnglishConnect 3 can help you develop advanced-level proficiency in Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing.

What Is Proficiency?

English proficiency is what you can DO with the language in unfamiliar situations.

To improve your proficiency, you must master four major language functions.

What Are the Four Language Functions?

To achieve advanced-level proficiency, you need to master these four functions:

  • Ask & Answer Questions
  • Describe
  • Narrate
  • Negotiate

How Does This Course Help Me Master the Four Major Language Functions?

This course includes a Weekly Gathering and online learning resources. The Weekly Gathering will focus on learning and practicing the four major language functions. The online learning resources are available on this site.

EnglishConnect 3 practice activities are organized by language skill: Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing. For each skill, you will practice and demonstrate mastery of the four major functions and their supporting can-do statements. As you increase your ability to do the four functions, you will move from intermediate to advanced-level English.

Where Do I Start?

EC3 has four levels in each skill – Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing. You will start at Level 1 in each skill. Your goal is to reach Level 4 in each skill.

  • Choose a skill to study – Listening, Speaking, Reading, or Writing.
  • Find your level.
  • Download the Workbook.
  • Read the Workbook introduction. Follow the instructions in the introduction. (GRAPHIC OF WORKBOOK INTRO)

  • For Listening practice, you will follow the links in the workbook to complete the activities and get immediate feedback.
  • For Reading practice, you can use the answers in your workbook to get immediate feedback. Consider why you chose the answer you selected, and why you were wrong or right.
  • For Speaking practice you will use one of the methods below, or another method you choose to send your audio file to the Reviewer. The Reviewer will give you feedback within 24 hours.

  • For Writing practice, you will follow the links in the workbook to complete the activities. The Reviewer will review your work and give you feedback within 24 hours.

How Do I Move to the Next Level?

  • To move to the next level, you must first achieve 75% on advanced-level practice items, and 90% on intermediate-level practice items.
  • As you complete the practice, track your scores on the practice items. (GRAPHIC OF SCORING PAGE) When you are consistently achieving 75% on the advanced items, and 90% on intermediate items, you are ready to take a Mastery Check.
  • When you pass the Mastery Check, you will go to the next level and begin the process again.

How Can I Be Successful?

  • You will be most successful as you prayerfully seek the gift of tongues, apply the six principles of being an agentive learner, and attend the Weekly Gathering.

Being an Agentive Learner

What Is an Agentive Learner?

An agentive learner chooses to act, and not be acted upon (2 N 2: 14-16). An agentive learner has a vision for who they will become, and consistently works toward that vision.

You can be an agentive learner. You are a child of God. You have divine nature and potential. As a child of God, you are endowed with power to act, and not be acted upon (2 N 2: 14-16). You can decide who you will become and what you will achieve in this life.

Learning English isn’t easy, but when you choose to be an agent of your own learning, you can accomplish your goals!

How Do I Improve My Ability to Be an Agent Learner?

Included below are six principles of being an agentive learner.

As you study, strive to apply the principles outlined below.

Principles of Being an Agentive Learner

  • Exercise Faith (Philippians 4:13)
  • Take Responsibility (2 N 2:14-16)
  • Teach One Another (D&C 88:77-79)
  • Press Forward (2 N 31:20)
  • Apply Effective Methods (D&C 109:7-9; 88:119)
  • Self-Evaluate (D&C 7:3-4)

Weekly Gathering

The Weekly Gathering is the most important part of this course, and will help you increase in faith and achieve your learning goals.

Each week, you will attend a Weekly Gathering with your classmates, and two Pathway Missionaries. At the Weekly Gathering you will practice English, and teach one another. Each week, a student will take the role of Lead Student. The Lead Student will follow a lesson plan to conduct the learning activities.
