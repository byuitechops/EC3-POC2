Getting Started

What Is EnglishConnect 3?

EnglishConnect 3 is an English course designed to help you achieve increased spiritual and temporal self-reliance. This course is designed to help you:

  • Increase your faith in Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ.
  • Become an agentive learner.
  • Increase your English proficiency to achieve your education and employment goals.

EnglishConnect 3 can help you develop intermediate-high level proficiency in Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing.

What Is Proficiency?

English proficiency is what you can DO with the language in new situations.

To improve your proficiency, you must master four major language functions.

What Are the Four Language Functions?

To achieve intermediate-high level proficiency, you need to master these four functions:

  • Ask & Answer Questions
  • Describe
  • Narrate
  • Negotiate

How Does This Course Help Me Master the Four Major Language Functions?

This course includes a Weekly Gathering and online learning resources. The Weekly Gathering will focus on learning and practicing the four major language functions. The online learning resources are available on this site.

EnglishConnect 3 practice activities are organized by language skill: Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing. For each skill, you will practice and develop mastery of the four major functions and their supporting can-do statements. As you increase your ability to do the four functions, you will move from intermediate-low to intermediate-high English proficiency.

Where Do I Start?

EnglishConnect 3 has fours skills - Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing. In each skill, there are four levels – Level 1, Level 2, Level 3, and Level 4. You will start at Level 1 in each skill. Your goal is to reach Level 4 in each skill.

  • Choose a skill to study - Listening, Speaking, Reading, or Writing.
  • Find your level.
  • Practice (GRAPHIC OF PRACTICE BUTTON)

How Do I Get Feedback?

You can get feedback on all of your practice activities.

  • Listening: You will get a score at the end of each topic. Think about your answers. Try to understand why your answers are right, or wrong.
  • Reading: You will get a score at the end of each topic. Think about your answers. Try to understand why your answers are right, or wrong.
  • Speaking: You must send your audio recording to the Reviewer.
    • Record yourself speaking using a voice recorder of your choice. Recommendations for voice recording tools include:
      • Use your phone to record. Attach the recording file in an email.
      • Talk & Comment - Paste the recording link into an email. If you do not know how to use this software, watch this video, Talk&Comment - Voice Notes anywhere (Chrome Tutorial) Video
      • Vocaroo- Paste the recording link into an email. If you do not know how to use this software, watch this video- Vocaroo Tutorial Video
    • Send your recording to your Reviewer in an email for feedback. (Reviewer email address: ec3@byui.edu)
    • The Reviewer will review your work and give you feedback within 24 hours during the week. On weekends, it may take 72 hours.
  • For Writing practice, you will follow the links in the workbook to complete the activities. The Reviewer will review your work and give you feedback within 24 hours. On weekends it may be 72 hours.

How Do I Move to the Next Level?

To move to the next level, you must first achieve 75% on intermediate-high level questions, and 90% on intermediate-low level questions.

As you complete the practice, track your scores.

When you are consistently achieving 75% on the intermediate-high questions, and 90% on intermediate-low level questions, you are ready to take a Mastery Check.

When you pass the Mastery Check, you will go to the next level and begin the process again.

How Can I Be Successful?

You will be most successful as you prayerfully seek the gift of tongues, apply the six principles of being an agentive learner, and attend the Weekly Gathering.

Being an Agentive Learner

What Is an Agentive Learner?

An agentive learner chooses to act, and not be acted upon (2 Nephi 2:14-16). An agentive learner has a vision for who they will become, and consistently works toward that vision.

You can be an agentive learner. You are a child of God. You have divine nature and potential. As a child of God, you are endowed with power to act, and not be acted upon (2 Nephi 2:14-16). You can decide who you will become and what you will achieve in this life.

Learning English isn’t easy, but when you choose to be an agent of your own learning, you can accomplish your goals!

Included below are six principles of being an agentive learner.

As you study, strive to apply the principles outlined below.

Principles of Being an Agentive Learner

  • Exercise Faith (Philippians 4:13)
  • Take Responsibility (2 Nephi 2:14-16)
  • Teach One Another (D&C 88:77-79)
  • Press Forward (2 Nephi 31:20)
  • Apply Effective Methods (D&C 109:7-9; 88:119)
  • Self-Evaluate (D&C 7:3-4)

How Do I Improve My Ability to Be an Agent Learner?

  • Study the six principles listed above.
  • Apply the principles in your daily study.
  • Each week, evaluate your progress. Use the progress tracker

Weekly Gathering

The Weekly Gathering is the most important part of this course, and will help you increase in faith and achieve your learning goals.

Each week, you will attend a Weekly Gathering with your classmates, and two Pathway Missionaries. At the Weekly Gathering you will practice English, and teach one another. Each week, a student will take the role of Lead Student. The Lead Student will follow a lesson plan to conduct the learning activities.

To prepare for the Weekly Gathering:

  • Download the Gathering Preparation Workbook
  • Read the introduction. Follow the instructions in the introduction.

If you are the Lead Student:

  • Download the Lead Student Gathering Workbook
  • Read the introduction. Follow the instructions in the introduction.
